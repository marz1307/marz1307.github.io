Analytics Engineer and Data Scientist. Manchester, UK. Open to Germany, EMEA, the UK, and remote.

Last updated April 2026

I spent the first half of my career being confused about why dashboards kept lying. Then I realised the dashboard was never the problem. It was always the pipeline upstream.

Fixing that has kept me busy ever since.

2015 / 2019

Banking, but the data side of it.

I started at Guaranty Trust Bank in Edo, Nigeria, on the customer-service side, where I learned the hard way that most business problems are data problems in disguise. People complained about the app. The app worked. The transactions were fine. What was broken was the data feeding into the complaint-resolution dashboard, which was throwing false positives because the CRM was de-duplicating records on a field it should not have been.

Fixing things like that turned into a job. I moved to the Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria in Abuja, Nigeria, as a Strategic Data Insights Analyst, working on loan portfolio analytics. Faulty data had been leaking into approval workflows. I rebuilt the integrity layer, wrote Python and SQL models to track repayments, and put Tableau dashboards into the governance team's hands. Approval times dropped 30 percent. Manual data entry dropped 25. Accuracy climbed 40 in six months.

2020 / 2023

Big data, in theory and in practice.

I moved to Istanbul, Türkiye, in 2020 to start my MSc in Big Data Analytics and Management at Bahcesehir University. Three years, 3.67 GPA, specialisation in big data engineering, market analytics, cloud, and Industry 4.0.

For my thesis I built a PySpark pipeline that ranked and recommended footballers across five European leagues using UEFA event data. The data came out at 500+ players across La Liga, Serie A, Premier League, Bundesliga and Ligue 1, plus the 2018 World Cup and Euro 2016. I engineered KPIs for on-ball and off-ball contribution, built a ranking layer, and used the result to surface scouting candidates by style-of-play signature rather than brute-force statistics. The thesis is in the Turkish National Thesis Centre and the code is on GitHub.

Alongside the MSc I got selected into Microsoft's Learn Student Ambassador programme, where I spent two years and nine months running Azure workshops, organising a hackathon that drew 50+ participants, and recruiting new ambassadors onto the programme.

2023 / 2025

Healthcare marketing, and a year of deliberate study.

After finishing the Bahcesehir MSc I spent 17 months deliberately widening the stack. Google Cloud, IBM, LinkedIn Learning. BigQuery ML. Python for Data Science. Tableau, Statistics Foundations 1 through 4, R for Data Science. Plus the IBM Enterprise Design Thinking practitioner track. I also open-sourced a PySpark and MLflow recommender system and a SQL Server airport-ticketing database design during that window.

In mid-2024 I joined Natural Clinic in Istanbul, Türkiye, as their CRM Data Specialist. The brief was clear: the CRM was a mess, and the marketing and sales teams did not trust the data. I rebuilt it from the ground up. Cleaned records, automated workflows, wired the CRM into the marketing stack, built live dashboards. Lead response time down 30 percent. Conversion up 15. Email engagement up 25. Duplicate records and errors down 40.

2025 / now

Second MSc and a live industry dissertation.

For my MSc Data Science dissertation I shipped the Account Intelligence Platform with Force24, a marketing automation SaaS based in Leeds, UK (Hybrid). A greenfield 16-week build for CSMs, accounts, and stakeholders, delivered collaboratively under technical supervision. Force24 specified PostgreSQL as the warehouse and Angular as the frontend stack. Every other tool decision was mine. I owned the data layer end to end: Dagster on a VM with Caddy auth, custom Python extractors against the source systems, every dbt model across raw, staging, intermediate, and marts, every Dagster job. I plugged that layer into the FastAPI service through endpoint changes and Redis caching, and contributed across the Angular frontend, where my lead feature was the Action Tracker. The Action Tracker is built on the principle that dashboards should drive action, not just display data. CSMs follow up with at-risk accounts using manual or smart-suggested actions surfaced by churn risk and account health score, admins distribute the work, and outcomes are captured as structured data that becomes a training set for a future causal model.

Alongside the Force24 work, my MSc dissertation at Salford was a separate, fully self-owned research surface: an agentic ELT data platform for customer intelligence, end to end. Custom Python extractors against multiple vendor APIs (over 1 million records ingested with zero silent loss), a 48-model dbt warehouse on PostgreSQL, a three-model churn intelligence stack (survival analysis with C-index 0.9449, XGBoost via PostgresML with SHAP at AUC 0.950, and DR-Learner causal inference correcting a naive +49.56pp CSM-effect down to an honest +12.64pp), and a Model Context Protocol endpoint exposing the whole layer to agentic LLM workflows. Surfaced through a FastAPI service with JWT and Row-Level Security and an Angular dashboard with SHAP explanations per account.

This is what the 2026 modern data stack actually looks like in production, compressed into one academic cycle. Documented, tested, defended in supervision, deployed against real API failure modes. It is the work I would point to first when someone asks about my data science range.

What comes next.

An Analytics Engineer or Data Scientist seat on a team that takes data quality seriously, ships to production, and wants to do more than rebuild last quarter's dashboard. I care about Kimball modelling, dbt, CI/CD for data, ML systems that explain themselves, and making analysts faster. I am happy on the messy plumbing nobody else wants to touch, because that is usually where the value is.

Germany is the medium-term goal. EMEA, the UK, or remote all work in the meantime.
